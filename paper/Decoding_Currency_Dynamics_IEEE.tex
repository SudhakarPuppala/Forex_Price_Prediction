\title{Decoding Currency Dynamics: A Hybrid CNN--LSTM--Transformer with Multi-Modal
Fusion and Honest Regime-Aware Evaluation for Multi-Step Gold Price Forecasting}

\author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Puppala V. V. Sudhakar}

\begin{abstract}
Foreign-exchange forecasting is difficult because prices are driven simultaneously by
price action, macroeconomic fundamentals and market sentiment, while classical models
such as ARIMA and GARCH assume the linearity and stationarity that currency data
routinely violate. We present \emph{Decoding Currency Dynamics}, an AI-driven framework
for multi-step forecasting of the XAU/USD (gold) exchange rate. Its core is a
\textbf{dual-tower Hybrid CNN--LSTM--Transformer} ($\approx$4.39\,M parameters) that fuses
three real data streams---twelve technical indicators, six stationary macroeconomic
variables, and thirteen FinBERT news-sentiment features---and predicts the next ten daily
log-returns together with a calibrated uncertainty band. A dilated causal CNN extracts
local structure, a presence-gated cross-attention module fuses sparse news into the price
representation, a Transformer supplies global context ahead of parallel Bi-LSTM/Bi-GRU
recurrence, and regime-conditioned probabilistic heads emit a mean and variance per
horizon under a Gaussian negative-log-likelihood objective. Four training techniques
---two-stage freeze-and-tune, probabilistic (GARCH-emulating) heads, modality masking and
deep supervision---stabilise learning on sparse, heterogeneous data. Evaluated by strict
walk-forward over 25.9 years of daily data (963 test origins, three seeds), the hybrid
reaches a directional accuracy of $0.534 \pm 0.001$ with the lowest mean absolute error
among deep configurations. We report results \emph{honestly}: an AR(1)--GARCH(1,1)
baseline still leads unfiltered directional accuracy ($0.575$), and a placebo-controlled
ablation isolates a genuine but modest ($\approx$1.4 percentage-point) contribution from a
news-diffusion feature, of which the majority of the naive gain is shown to be an
added-channel effect. The hybrid's advantages are multi-modal reach, native uncertainty
quantification and a conviction-based decision layer rather than raw accuracy.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Foreign-exchange (FX) markets are among the most liquid and volatile financial markets,
influenced by macroeconomic indicators, geopolitical events and market sentiment.
Traditional forecasting approaches such as the Box--Jenkins ARIMA framework~\cite{box}
and Bollerslev's GARCH model~\cite{garch} remain foundational for autocorrelation and
volatility clustering, but both presuppose stationarity and linear dependence structures
that high-frequency currency data violate, motivating a shift toward non-linear,
data-driven and increasingly multi-modal architectures.

\emph{Multi-step} forecasting is especially hard: errors compound across horizons, and
most prior deep-learning studies report only single-step accuracy. This paper targets the
harder multi-horizon problem for gold (XAU/USD), a liquid and strongly macro-sensitive
instrument. Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \textbf{dual-tower Hybrid CNN--LSTM--Transformer} that unifies convolutional
spatial extraction, recurrent temporal modelling, Transformer global attention and FinBERT
sentiment fusion in a single end-to-end, regime-aware, probabilistic architecture.
\item A \textbf{multi-modal feature-fusion pipeline} that aligns technical, macroeconomic
and news-sentiment streams onto a common daily grid, with a presence gate that keeps the
model robust when news is absent (the common case).
\item Four \textbf{training techniques}---two-stage freeze-and-tune, Gaussian-NLL
probabilistic heads, modality masking and deep supervision---that make training stable on
sparse, heterogeneous data.
\item An \textbf{honest, leakage-free evaluation protocol}: full walk-forward against
ARIMA and GARCH at every test origin, a placebo-controlled ablation, and explicit
reporting of where the classical baseline still wins.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
Hochreiter and Schmidhuber's Long Short-Term Memory~\cite{lstm} introduced the gating
mechanism that underpins nearly every sequential financial forecaster; Vaswani \emph{et
al.}~\cite{transformer} showed that self-attention captures long-range dependencies
without recurrence. Recent IEEE-indexed work converges on themes directly relevant here.
Benchmarks of LSTM, gradient-boosted trees and Transformers on multiple currency
pairs~\cite{forexlstm} conclude that no single architecture dominates all regimes and that
an ensemble balances accuracy and reliability, motivating a hybrid rather than
single-model design. Sequential deep-learning studies that fuse windowed LSTM encoders
with news sentiment~\cite{seqdl} outperform ARIMA baselines, reinforcing the value of
pairing sequence models with exogenous text. On the spatial front, converting price
series into image-like representations for a convolutional classifier~\cite{cnnforex}
achieves high directional accuracy on out-of-sample data, justifying a CNN local-feature
stage. Most directly, augmenting an econometric FX model with a FinBERT~\cite{finbert}
sentiment index~\cite{newsfx} shows the index behaves as a leading indicator---the
principal precedent for our sentiment backbone. Three gaps persist: these streams are
usually treated as separate pipelines rather than one unified model; multi-step forecasting
is rarely addressed; and evaluation seldom isolates behaviour by volatility regime. Our
design targets all three.

\section{Data and Feature Engineering}
\subsection{Data sources}
Daily XAU/USD candles are obtained from a public market feed (ticker \texttt{GC=F}),
giving 6{,}487 bars spanning 2000--2026 ($\approx$25.9 years). Macroeconomic variables are
sourced from public providers---short-rate and Treasury-yield proxies, the trade-weighted
dollar index, and Consumer-Price-Index releases. Financial news headlines are collected
from a free news API and scored for sentiment. The pipeline is \emph{incremental} and
\emph{cached}: news is fetched only since the last run and each headline is FinBERT-scored
once, so historical text is never re-scored.

\subsection{Three aligned streams (31 features)}
All streams are computed over an identical 60-bar lookback and fused into a per-bar vector.
\emph{Technical (12):} OHLC log-returns, RSI, MACD histogram, Bollinger-band width,
volume $z$-score, ATR\%, rate-of-change, stochastic \%K and an EMA ratio. \emph{Macro (6):}
short-rate $z$-score, 10-year yield change, dollar-index return, CPI year-over-year and
month-over-month, and days-since-CPI. \emph{Sentiment (13):} nine rolling FinBERT
statistics---including a \emph{diffusion breadth} index---plus a one-hot
buy/sell/hold/none trading signal. All features use stationary transforms and are
normalised with statistics fit on the training split only.

\subsection{FinBERT sentiment scoring}
FinBERT~\cite{finbert}, a BERT-family transformer pretrained on financial text, emits a
three-way distribution over \{positive, neutral, negative\} per headline. Following
industry practice, we aggregate only \emph{directional} headlines
($|\text{polarity}| \ge 0.15$, confidence-weighted) so that the neutral majority does not
dilute the signal, and we derive the buy/sell trading signal with an \emph{adaptive},
regime-relative threshold (an expanding, causal $z$-score) rather than a fixed cut. A
diffusion breadth index $(\#\text{bullish} - \#\text{bearish})/\#\text{total}$ captures
directional agreement independently of magnitude.

\section{Model Architecture}
The network (Fig.~\ref{fig:arch}) is a dual-tower design. Tensor shapes are given for batch
size $B$ and lookback $T=60$.

\subsection{Tower A---quantitative pathway}
The 18 technical+macro features $(B,60,18)$ are projected to width 64 and passed to a
\textbf{dilated causal CNN}: three stacked convolution blocks with dilations $1/2/4$ and
left-padding (WaveNet/TCN style), \emph{no pooling}, preserving full 60-bar resolution
$\rightarrow (B,60,128)$. A learned volatility-regime embedding is added.

\subsection{Tower B---news pathway}
The 13 sentiment features $(B,60,13)$ are encoded by a GRU and projected to
$(B,60,128)$.

\subsection{Presence-gated cross-attention fusion}
Multi-head cross-attention uses the price stream as Query and the news stream as
Key/Value. A per-timestep \textbf{presence gate}
$g_t=\sigma(W_g\,\mathbf{s}_t)$ scales the news contribution on a residual path, so on
news-less bars the gate closes and the model falls back to price/macro:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{f}_t = \mathrm{LN}\!\left(\mathbf{q}_t + g_t\cdot \mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{q}_t,\mathbf{K},\mathbf{V})\right).
\end{equation}

\subsection{Transformer, recurrence and pooling}
The fused sequence is projected to $d_\text{model}=256$ and passed through a Transformer
encoder with sinusoidal positional encoding and a causal mask $\rightarrow (B,60,256)$;
global attention is placed \emph{before} recurrence to avoid recency bias. Parallel
\textbf{Bi-LSTM $\parallel$ Bi-GRU} branches are blended by a learned gate and
attention-pooled to a context vector $(B,256)$.

\subsection{Regime-aware probabilistic output and fused expert}
A volatility-regime detector routes the context to regime-conditioned decoder heads that
emit a mean $\mu_h$ and log-variance $\log\sigma_h^2$ for each of the ten horizons. A
frozen \textbf{XGBoost}~\cite{xgboost} expert is fused through a regime-driven per-horizon
trust gate:
\begin{equation}
\hat{y}_h = \tau_h\odot \hat{y}^{\text{xgb}}_h + (1-\tau_h)\odot \hat{y}^{\text{deep}}_h .
\end{equation}

\section{Training Methodology}
\subsection{Two-stage freeze-and-tune}
Because news exists for only a fraction of history, training the text tower end-to-end
would project many news-less years of zeroed sentiment onto the fusion. \emph{Stage 1}
trains the quantitative backbone text-free over the full history; \emph{Stage 2} freezes
that backbone ($\approx$4.15\,M parameters) and fine-tunes only the text tower, fusion and
heads ($\approx$0.24\,M parameters) on the news-dense recent subset at $0.1\times$ the
learning rate.

\subsection{Gaussian NLL (probabilistic) heads}
Each head predicts a distribution, trained under
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\text{NLL}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left(\log\sigma^2 + \frac{(y-\mu)^2}{\sigma^2}\right),
\end{equation}
emulating GARCH's conditional-variance modelling inside the network. The predicted
$\sigma$ yields an uncertainty band and a conviction statistic $|\mu|/\sigma$ that powers a
calibrated abstention rule and a costed backtest.

\subsection{Modality masking and deep supervision}
The sentiment stream is randomly zeroed with probability $p=0.4$ during training so the
model cannot over-rely on frequently-absent news; this complements the inference-time
presence gate. An auxiliary head on a deeper expert (weight $0.5$) injects gradient signal
into the middle of the deep stack, improving flow and stabilising training. A robust Huber
term and a small directional loss complete the objective.

\section{Experimental Setup}
Data are split chronologically (70/15/15). Normalisation statistics are fit on the training
split only. Both classical baselines---ARIMA (walk-forward) and AR(1)--GARCH(1,1)---are
\emph{refit at every one of the 963 test origins} the hybrid is scored on. We report the
mean $\pm$ standard deviation across three seeds (9, 36, 99). Primary metrics are
directional accuracy (sign agreement across all horizons), mean absolute error (MAE) and
RMSE on cumulative log-returns; we additionally report a placebo-controlled ablation, a
seed ensemble and a costed conviction backtest.

\subsection{Ablation with placebo control}
Table~\ref{tab:abl} isolates the sentiment \emph{diffusion} feature using a same-seed A/B
plus a placebo: replacing the feature with a random permutation of its own values (same
distribution, signal destroyed). The naive $+3.4$~pp gain decomposes into $\approx$2.0~pp
of added-input-channel effect (achieved even by noise) and $\approx$1.4~pp of genuine
diffusion signal---a small but real, seed-stable contribution.

\subsection{Conviction machinery}
The seed ensemble reaches $0.534$ directional accuracy overall and $0.549$ at 10\% coverage
(its most-confident calls). The probabilistic conviction signal drives a costed
1-step strategy that returns $+61\%$ net at an annualised Sharpe of $0.76$ over the test
window---evidence that the model's \emph{uncertainty} is informative even where its
\emph{unfiltered} accuracy is not.

\subsection{Honest discussion}
On this strongly trending, volatility-clustered daily series, GARCH's AR(1)
conditional-mean drift is a genuinely strong directional baseline that the hybrid does not
overtake on raw accuracy. A feature-attribution analysis attributes the forecast largely
to technical/price features ($\approx$70--82\%) and macro ($\approx$18--30\%), with news
sentiment contributing little---consistent with the sparse news coverage ($\approx$14--18\%
of bars). The hybrid's value is therefore its \emph{breadth and decision quality}:
multi-modal inputs, learned non-linear structure, native per-horizon uncertainty, regime
awareness, and a conviction layer that turns forecasts into a positive costed strategy.

\section{Limitations}
News coverage is the binding constraint: with only $\approx$14--18\% of test bars carrying
headlines, the sentiment stream cannot express its full value, and denser coverage via a
keyed news provider is the primary lever for improvement. Results are for a single
instrument (XAU/USD) at daily cadence. Finally, we emphasise that directional accuracy
above $\approx$0.6 is not attainable on this task without look-ahead leakage; we report our
figures as such rather than pursuing inflated numbers.

\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
We presented a reproducible, multi-modal Hybrid CNN--LSTM--Transformer that unifies
convolutional, recurrent, attention and sentiment pathways into one regime-aware,
probabilistic forecaster, and evaluated it honestly against classical baselines with a
placebo-controlled ablation. The framework closes the three literature gaps---unified
multi-modal design, explicit multi-step forecasting, and regime-aware evaluation---while
being transparent about where a strong econometric baseline still wins. Future work will
(i) integrate a keyed, denser news source to unlock the sentiment stream, (ii) add
explainable-AI attributions (SHAP, attention analysis), (iii) conduct broader ablations and
multi-pair generalisation, and (iv) wrap the probabilistic forecaster in a reinforcement-
learning execution agent.
